## Purpose

Player-character state, personal history anchors, Ratings, wounds, Dooms, and carried items.

## Character Overview

- Name: Honorine Calliste
- Concept: Perceptive Sage who Serves the Green
- Ordinary life: Healer drawing on formal Western medicine, bayou herbalism, and spiritist practice from a base at Crossrow outside New Orleans
- Arrival into the woods: Went deeper into the bayou seeking protective herbs to stave off the Something's return and became utterly lost; on a deeper level, she was drawn by loneliness, fear, and the need for an answer
- Primary motivation: Find a source of power in the woods strong enough to defend herself from the Something that has noticed her, and possibly protect the patient whose healing first drew that attention

## Isolation And Loss

- Core loneliness or estrangement: Publicly cast under suspicion as a witch after saving a powerful preacher's son; increasingly cut off from her town, from ordinary trust, and from certainty that her gift is good
- Unresolved loss: Loss of belonging, safety, and spiritual confidence after the miracle at Crossrow
- Lost loved one: Muriel, the preacher's wife, Honorine's secret lover, who died from complications after giving birth to Cal
- Important past figures:
  - Cal, the preacher's young son, brought back from the brink by a ritual that touched Something vast
  - The influential preacher, who privately sought her aid and then publicly disavowed her
  - Muriel, whose memory still binds love, grief, jealousy, longing, and guilt together

## Emotional Truths

- Honorine still grieves Muriel years after her death.
- She loves Cal and yet has secretly wished that he had died in Muriel's place.
- She feels deep guilt for that wish.
- She once felt pity for the preacher, but his betrayal has turned that pity into white-hot rage.
- Honorine knows that Cal's name carries her own surname in secret, not only John Calvin's.
- Cal has Muriel's pond-green eyes, which makes him harder to hate and harder to bear.

## Belief And Faith

- Honorine believes her power comes from the Green.
- She does not reject the preacher's God, but sees him as a shallow, rigid, diminished understanding of a holiness that is fuller and more alive in the bayou.
- She believes the preacher mistakes narrowness for righteousness because he has never truly seen the sacred depth of living things.
- Until Cal's healing, she understood the Green as life, growth, healing, and sustaining power.
- What answered her in the ritual felt instead like darkness, rot, hunger, and selfishness.
- She is now spiritually shaken by the possibility that this too may be part of the Green.
- She must know whether she touched something outside the Green, or an aspect of it she has never before dared to face.

### Active And Passive Abilities

- Charmed Touch:
  - Cypher form: costs 1 Intellect, heals 1d6 to one stat Pool, difficulty 2 Intellect task, repeated use on the same creature grows harder until that creature rests
  - Darkest System interpretation: Honorine can attempt a healing action against a wound or harmful condition using Rating 2; repeated attempts on the same subject should become more difficult until that subject has had substantial rest
- See the Unseen:
  - Automatically perceives magically invisible, insubstantial, or hidden creatures/objects
  - Searching for more conventionally hidden things is eased
- Living Off the Land:
  - Can reliably find edible food, usable herbs, and potable water in wilderness
  - Trained to resist natural poisons
  - Immune to natural disease

### Interpretation Notes For Darkest System

- Honorine is physically vulnerable compared with more martial characters.
- Her strongest practical edges are perception, medicine, herbs, wilderness survival, and occult working.
- She should feel competent in healing and uncanny observation, but not built for direct frontal violence.
