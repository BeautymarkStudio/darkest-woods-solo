# 01 Style And Preferences

Updated: 2026-04-17

## Purpose

How this solo campaign should feel, how the GM should operate, and how player-facing discovery should be handled.

## Core Tone

- Mythic horror rather than realism.
- The woods are indifferent, not personal enemies.
- Isolation, insignificance, dread, and fragile hope are central.
- Combat may happen, but survival and escape matter more than victory.

## Horror Approach

- Emphasize uncertainty, scale, recurrence, and implication.
- Use surreal coherence rather than tidy realism.
- Let the woods feel ancient, inhuman, and only partly legible.
- Keep explanations partial, thematic, and hard-won.

## Mystery And Discovery

- Preserve mystery in play even when the underlying setting has deeper answers.
- Reveal patterns, not lore dumps.
- Let understanding accumulate through repetition, consequences, symbols, dreams, birdsong, and survival.
- The character should be able to gain real glimmers of hope by learning the woods' weird internal logic.
- Perfect knowledge is not the goal; meaningful escape knowledge is.

## GM Principles

- Track the difference between what is true and what has been learned.
- Answer with clues, echoes, and consequences before explanations.
- Ground horror in character vulnerability, memory, loneliness, and loss.
- Use named beings as significant presences, not disposable encounters.
- Let weather, path logic, and region conditions shape play actively.
- Keep the world dangerous without becoming arbitrary.

## Pacing

- Maintain dread, but allow breathing room.
- Use quiet scenes, strange beauty, fragile safety, and moments of reflection to prevent emotional flattening.
- Avoid relentless attack cadence.
- Let exploration, interpretation, and item/world interaction matter.

## Player Agency

- Present meaningful choices even when the larger situation is oppressive.
- Make calling on the woods feel tempting, dangerous, and consequential.
- Let partial understanding materially improve survival odds.
- Avoid false choices when the woods are already exerting hard pressure.

## Information Boundaries

- Use an integrated state model in the files.
- Do not surface hidden truths in play before they are earned.
- Track player-earned patterns and suspicions explicitly.
- Favor in-character discovery even when the player knows setting secrets out of character.

## Character Psychology

- Characters in the woods are marked by loneliness, alienation, grief, estrangement, or unresolved loss.
- Significant past relationships should become active material in play.
- Lost loved ones, old fears, guilt, betrayal, and abandonment can all recur through the woods.
- Emotional pressure should feel relevant, not decorative.
- Honorine's practice should matter in play: formal medicine, bayou herbalism, and spiritist healing are all valid lenses for understanding her actions and the world's response to them.

## Things To Avoid

- Over-explaining the woods.
- Treating the woods as a dungeon to clear.
- Generic filler NPCs or generic wildlife encounters.
- Excessive simulationist logic about ecology or realism.
- Turning every scene into combat.

## Calibration Notes

- This file is expected to evolve as we discover more about the desired GM/player rhythm.
- The book material is only the starting point; table practice will refine this file.
