# 00 Rules

Updated: 2026-04-16

## Purpose

Concise reference for the Darkest System as used in this solo campaign.

## Core Principles

- Everything meaningful has a `Rating` from 1 to 10.
- Ratings are treated as absolute unless we intentionally note otherwise.
- The system is story-first and conversion is narrative, not formulaic.
- The player always rolls.

## Action Resolution

- Roll `2d6 + relevant Rating`.
- Target number is `7 + task Rating`.
- If the task is `6+` above the acting Rating, it is normally impossible.
- If the task is `6+` below the acting Rating, it is normally impossible to fail.

## The Darkest Die

- Every action roll also includes the `Darkest Die`.
- The Darkest Die does not normally affect success or failure.
- If the Darkest Die is higher than either of the two kept action dice, the woods act.
- A player may call on the woods and add the Darkest Die to the result.
- Calling on the woods always causes a transgression event and gains `1 Doom`.

## Boons And Banes

- `Boon`: roll an extra `d6` and keep the highest two regular dice.
- `Bane`: roll an extra `d6` and keep the lowest two regular dice.
- Multiple Boons/Banes do not add more dice, but they do cancel each other.
- Circumstances usually change rolls by Boons/Banes, not by changing Ratings.

## Special Success

- If one die by itself plus the relevant Rating meets or beats the target number, the action is a special success.
- In combat, a special success usually means double damage or a strong situational effect.

## Damage And Wounds

- Damage roll: `1d6 + attack Rating - defense/resistance Rating`.
- If the result is positive, that value is the `wound Rating`.
- If the result is `0` or less, there is no meaningful wound.
- Physical and mental wounds are tracked separately.
- Damage rolls do not use the Darkest Die.

## PC Wounds

- Each wound gives a `Bane`, though Banes do not stack beyond canceling Boons.
- When a wounded PC takes a new wound, they must roll against their highest current wound.
- On a failure, they fall unconscious or are otherwise unable to act.
- An unconscious PC with a wound rated higher than their own Rating must later roll against their highest wound, subtracting current Dooms.
- Failure on that roll means death from physical injury or a similarly final collapse if the situation warrants.
- Surviving that critical roll removes `1 Doom`.

## NPC Wounds

- Track the Ratings of wounds dealt to an NPC.
- An NPC is defeated when total wound Ratings reach `3 x NPC Rating`.
- A single wound rated `3+` above the NPC's own Rating defeats them immediately.
- Tough special foes may use a higher defeat threshold if needed.

## Healing

- A few minutes of safe rest allows a roll against each current wound.
- Success clears that wound.
- Failure means that wound cannot be healed again by ordinary rest for `24 hours`.
- Medical attention allows another healing attempt without waiting.
- Magical healing usually removes a wound outright.
- Sleeping `6-8` hours removes the lowest-rated wound automatically, but sleep in the woods is dangerous.

## Sleep In The Woods

- Sleep is a choice, not a necessity.
- Sleep usually brings nightmares and fitful rest.
- After `3` such sleeps, each further sleep requires resisting a `Rating 3` mental attack.

## Transgressions

- A transgression occurs when the Darkest Die is highest on an action roll.
- Certain actions are also always transgressions: leaving the path, flying or otherwise taking to the air, traversing the same path two days in a row, killing an animal without gratitude, and destroying a large section of the woods.
- Transgressions advance the current region's witch event track.

## Movement And Space

- Paths stabilize space; moving off the path breaks normal spatial logic.
- Leaving the path causes a transgression and sends the traveler elsewhere in the woods.
- Region transitions and spatial connections are not intuitive.

## Time, Language, And Survival

- All intelligent beings can understand each other in the woods.
- Days and nights exist, but longer calendars matter less.
- Food is usually not required after the first day or two.
- No contact with the outside world is possible from within the woods.

## Escape

- Escape requires learning the woods' internal logic rather than mastering the whole place.
- The main route out depends on discovering and using the Birdsong Path.

## Campaign-Specific Notes

- We are using the Darkest System directly rather than converting to another RPG.
- Mystery is preserved in play, but growing partial understanding is a core reward.
- Weather and region conditions are active parts of play and world state.
